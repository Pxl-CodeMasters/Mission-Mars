    Mission Mars

    ->

    Timeline

    The timeline of Mission M.A.R.S. Scroll down to explore! Hope you enjoy the story! NOTE: This is a fictional representation, not all situations might be realistic...

    Year: 2014

    The Beginning Of Our End

    -By the year 2020, It has been evident that Earth will not be a favorable long-term home. NASA predicts that by 2050 Earth will be just too 'full' & there won't be any more land for growing crops or doing almost anything that is possible today.... It is obvious that we would some day or the other have to migrate from Earth and start living life on another planet....

    But Where??

    But the big question is where? Where do we have enough oxygen? Where do we have enough water? Which is the closest planet we could colonize? The most popular answer is either Mars or our satellite Moon... But which one is more preferable? The Moon has some serious advantages over Mars, and Mars has some serious advantages over the Moon. Obviously the Moon is much more closer to the Earth and so shipping things from the Earth would be much more cheaper. But Mars is much better for a 'long-term habitat'. It would definitely be more expensive and slower to colonize mars and lead a normal Earth-like life....

    Year: 2015

    The First Steps

    By now, people were fascinated by the idea of colonizing another planet, especially one fine man, Elon Musk. He was absolutely astonished by this idea and took his own steps into making this a reality. He & his team at SpaceX started to design a rocket that would take colonists & supplies to mars. He named this rocket 'Starship'. This same year Russian space agency Roscosmos successfully sent a probe into the orbit of Mars. Well scientists discovered that Mars surface wasn't really suitable for human life. Humans can't live on Mars without space suits....

    Year: 2018

    The First Experiments

    -To colonize Mars, we would have to know much more about it so, NASA sent a robot there. The NASA InSight Lander was rover that was sent to Mars in 5th May 2018. Its goal was to uncover how a rocky body forms and evolves to become a planet by investigating the interior structure and composition of Mars.

    Year: 2019

    A Few Prototypes Here & A Few There

    SpaceX has been spending years designing their Starship. Finally in 2019, The Starhopper a prototype version of the Starship. The aim of this prototype was to fly Starhopper to about 500 feet altitude. The company built the enormous rocket to achieve its goal of transporting up to 100 people in the space flights. SpaceX begins to build more prototypes aiming to send the first humans to Mars.

    Year: 2021

    Mission Concept by Space X

    SpaceX has proposed the development of Mars transportation infrastructure in order to facilitate the eventual colonization of Mars.The mission architecture includes fully reusable launch vehicles, human-rated spacecraft, on-orbit propellant tankers, rapid-turnaround launch/landing mounts, and local production of rocket fuel on Mars via In-Situ Resource Utilization (ISRU). SpaceX's aspirational goal as of 2017 was to land their cargo Starships on Mars by 2024 and the first 2 crewed starships by 2026.

    Exploring The Deep

    This was a remarkable year for space exploration. NASA's Perseverance Rover successfully landed on Mars. SpaceX completed a high-altitude test flight & landing of their latest Starship prototype. NASA picked 3 companies to build the first human lunar lander model one of them being SpaceX. Later on, China would land their own lander & rover on the surface of Mars....

    Year: 2023

    The Race Is On

    By now more and more countries blast into the scene. India will be launching Mangalyaan 2 to test the Martian atmosphere & the surface from orbit using its 5 science instruments. While, Japan will launch its MMX (Martian Moons Explorer) to get samples from Mars' moon Phobos and send them back to Earth for further studying. But SpaceX is much more ahead in the run, the Starship prototype is now ready and operating crewed test flights. The first orbital flight is a success....

    Year: 2026

    Lunar Missions

    SpaceX is back again but this time, its a flight to the Moon. The #dearmoon mission will take its crew on an orbit around the Moon. At the same time, an uncrewed SpaceX ship will be set to land on the lunar south pole which will be the most significant landing for the company yet. But the most notable development was going elsewhere. The main structure of the Lunar Gateway is complete. This small space station will orbit the Moon and be sort of a gas station for future space programmes....

    Year: 2028

    The Big Test & Another Leap For Mankind

    The biggest test is now here, SpaceX has finally finished with their spaceship. The ship has been tested, but this is going to be its longest trip yet. Hence, they decided to make their first mission to Mars, unmanned. The two cargo Starships will be heading to Mars, they both are going to be loaded with a mini nuclear power reactor, and a few other devices to create Oxygen & Methane from the Martian atmosphere which would make life possible. This is around the same time when NASA will be retrieving samples from Mars & transport it back to the Earth. This is apparently also the same year in which SpaceX plans to send humans to the Moon to search for frozen water in its polar regions....

    Year: 2030

    The Start Of A Civilization

    Now that the Lunar Starship's test is successfull, several more programmes will be coming. One of them aims to establish the first human base on the moon - The Artemis Base Camp, which will allow the astronauts to stick around for longer periods & do research on-site. There still aren't any plans to create permanent accomodation on the Moon. While all this is going on, ground tests from Mars will be back which allow SpaceX to finally pick the location for the Mars Base Alpha, the first human outpost on Mars....

    Year: 2032

    The Foundation

    Well Mars is about to get populated but there still many humans in the newest missions. Two unmanned Starships arrive on the future site of Mars Base Alpha, and toss out a fleet of rovers, solar panels & mining robots that will build the foundation of the base. It'll be one of the biggest construction projects in the history of mankind. It would approximately take more than year to complete....

    Year: 2034

    Civilizations On The Moon??

    The robots are doing a great job, as landing fields are now cleared and the base continues to be prepared. But while all this 'architecture' work is going on, the Moon is about to become alot more crowded. Artemis Base Camp was completed recently. And after a few tests, it is ready to welcome its first residents. Just like the ISS (International Space Station) this base will allow its residents to conduct long term study of the Moon....

    Year: 2036

    The Countdown Is Over.

    "We've come along way, now the entire world is watching us" Elon Musk said. SpaceX has recruited 24 lucky individuals to become the crew members of the first manned mission to Mars. The crew will be divided into groups of twelve, and sent on a pair of Starships followed by cargo ships. While this is primarily a SpaceX mission, they are backed up by NASA and will be watched by everyone around the globe. The cargo ships are crucial for this mission as they will be carrying a ISRU. This machine will be used to collect, proccess, store, and use the materials found on Mars to replace construction supplies and propellants that would otherwise need to be taken and carried from the Earth....

    Time For Liftoff

    Solar panels are also being constructed around the planet by robots, providing much of the power Mars Base Alpha will be using. SpaceX also deploys its Starlink satellites around Mars, guaranteeing communication and GPS abilities on this new horizon. Well, certainly no one would want to get lost in the new planet. 'But would it work?' that's the question everyone wants to know. SpaceX estimates the trip to Mars will still take a few months but it will be faster than the NASA voyages. After a few years, the Starships touch down on Mars. The whole world watches....

    Year: 2038

    Finding Life

    Now the hard work begins, the 24 residents of the Mars Base Alpha are living in a self-contained environment, in an artificial atmosphere, and only travel outside the base for research missions under controlled situations in space suits that are designed to withstand the harsh conditions outside. The robots have been scouting for the presence of frozen water for years and once the best location has been found out, it'll be mined and processed into drinkable water. The construction robots are still at hard work - building more landing and launch pads for future Starships....
